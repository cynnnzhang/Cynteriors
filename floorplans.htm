FLOORPLANS

This is where you will learn how to use PlanningWiz to create 2D and 3D floor plans!

Floor plans are super useful to create a detailed plan of your room. There are many advanced floor plans softwares that cost money. However, on this page I will teach you how to use PlanningWiz, a simple free software program online.

Step 1: Opening the Application

Click here to go to PlanningWiz

Once you open the software, you will be given options like opening a saved design, uploading a floor plan, etc. But in this tutorial I will be showing you how to start from scratch!

Step 2: Creating your Room

Start by using the Draw Room function in the tool bar to create your room. If you wish, you may change the dimensions of the walls. Next, you can create the flooring for the room using the Draw Floor function and pinning the four corners. Double click when you are done drawing to end it.

To change the colour or texture of your flooring, click the floor, then click the little paintbrush in the top right corner. There you will find different textures, like parquet, tile, carpet, color, etc. Choose whatever texture you like and drag it into the main square in the upper right.

Step 3: Inserting Elements

The left column is filled with options for furniture, floors, doors and windows, and there are also predefined plans if you don't want to start bare bones.

Click and drag to insert objects into your room; you can adjust them to fit using the rotate and sizing buttons. To see what the objects look in 3D first, click the 3D option. Items that have color changing options will be indicated in the botttom right corner!

Step 4: Viewing your Design

There are 5 viewing modes: 2D, 3D, Animate, Walkthrough, and PIP. 3D is the base mode while the others are additional (Except PIP).

3D View: Will allow you to view in 3D. Use the zoom and arrow functions on the right to move around and view from different angles.

Animate 3D View: This mode will rotate your room in 3D automatically.

Walkthrough 3D View: This mode will allow you to essentially "walk through" your room from a humans view point. Press Esc button to escape.

PIP (Picture-in-picture) 3D View: This mode will allow you to edit in 2D, while also viewing the 3D version in a small window.
